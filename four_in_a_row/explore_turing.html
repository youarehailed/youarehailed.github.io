We recruited 30 participants to play against AI opponents for 60 minutes. On a second session, they watched 180 videos of segments of games either between two human players, or two computers that follow a model with parameters fitted to those human players. After watching the segment, participants guess whether the players were human or computers, and report their subjective confidence in that judgement. Participants received correctness feedback after each trial. We compensated participants $12 per session plus $6 completion bonus.
